About me

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte , working under the supervision of Dr. Pu Wang at the GENIUS Lab . My research focuses on advancing computer vision and generative AI, particularly in 3D Human Pose Estimation and Mesh Recovery. Moreover, I am interested in 3D Human Motion Generation driven by multi-modal inputs such as text or music, focusing on achieving controllability and high-quality synthesis to produce realistic human motions.

Previously, at the Image Privacy Lab under Dr. Liyue Fan , I developed privacy-preserving federated frameworks for generative AI, enabling secure synthetic data generation in non-IID settings. Before joining UNCC, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Lahore University of Management Sciences (LUMS) in August 2021. There, I worked with Dr. Fareed Zaffar and Dr. Agha Ali Raza at the Center for Speech and Language Technologies (CSaLT) .

Research Interests

My research interests lie in Computer Vision and Generative AI. Specifically, I focus on:

  • 3D Human Pose Estimation and Mesh Recovery
  • Generative AI, particularly masked modeling-based multi-modality generation and synthesis
  • Multi-Camera Multi-Person Tracking
  • Vision-Language Models for Human Understanding
